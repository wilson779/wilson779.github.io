Introducing Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso (Spanish: Pablo Picasso, October 25, 1881~April 8, 1973), The full name is Pablo Diego Jos é Francisco de Paula Juan Nepomuseno Mar í a de los Remedios Cipriano de la Sant í sima Trinidad Ruiz Picasso, a Spanish painter, sculptor and member of the French Communist Party. He is the founder of modern art and the main representative of western modernist painting. Picasso is the most creative and far-reaching artist in the contemporary West, and one of the greatest artistic talents in the 20th century. Representative works: Guernica, Dove of Peace, Yaweinong Girl and Life.

Picasso was born in Malaga, Spain, in 1881. In 1897, Picasso went to the Prado Art Museum in Madrid for further study. In 1900, Picasso went to Paris to work and live. In 1903, he completed his painting Life. In 1907, the completion of the painting "Maiden of Avignon" became a milestone in his creation of Cubist style. In 1937, Picasso created a large painting "Guernica" to commemorate the Nazi bombing of Guernica. In 1949, his work "Peace Pigeon" was selected as the poster of the International Peace Conference. In 1973, Picasso died in Muren, France, at the age of 91.
